Data Usage & Privacy

ISS Spotter uses location information only to calculate when the International Space Station (ISS) may be visible from your sky.

You may choose how your location is provided. You can allow browser-based location access, search for a city or place, or manually enter geographic coordinates (latitude and longitude). Granting browser location access is optional and not required to use the application.

Only the last location you provide is stored. ISS Spotter does not track your movement in real time and does not collect location history.

Your email address is used solely for authentication and, if you explicitly enable notifications, to send alerts on days when the ISS is expected to be visible from your saved location. You may enable or disable notifications at any time from your profile.

ISS Spotter does not sell, share, or monetize user data, does not perform background tracking, and does not collect information unrelated to ISS visibility and user account functionality.

You remain in full control of your data. You may update or delete your saved location, delete stored user data, or delete your account entirely at any time.
