ABOUT ISS SPOTTER

Why I Built This

One evening after sunset, I was sitting in my balcony when I noticed something moving very fast across the sky.

It wasn’t a shooting star. It wasn’t an airplane. It moved steadily. That’s when I thought — could it be a satellite?

What’s the biggest object in space that’s easily visible from Earth? The International Space Station.

I checked an ISS tracking website to confirm — and it was indeed the ISS. That moment genuinely made me happy.

Then I thought — what if I could track it myself? What if I could get notified before the next sighting?

That curiosity led to building ISS Spotter.

How to Use ISS Spotter

  • Sign in with Google.
  • Set your location (browser, city search, or manual coordinates).
  • The dashboard shows your next visible ISS pass.
  • View the ISS live position and orbit track on the map.
  • Enable email notifications from your profile.

Pass predictions are refreshed automatically in the backend every few hours.

Public APIs & Libraries Used

  • ISS Live Data – WhereTheISS.at API
  • Map Tiles – OpenStreetMap
  • City Search – Nominatim API
  • Orbital Calculations – satellite.js

Grateful to these open APIs and libraries that make space data accessible.

Privacy Note

ISS Spotter is designed with minimal data collection, transparency, and full user control. Below is a detailed explanation of how your data is handled.

What we store:
• Your Google display name and email address (for authentication and communication)
• The last location you provide (latitude and longitude), used only to calculate ISS visibility
• Your notification preference (whether you choose to receive emails or not)

How location is provided:
• You may allow browser-based location access, search for a city or place, or manually enter coordinates
• Granting browser location permission is optional and not required to use the application
• Only the most recent location you provide is stored
• We do not collect location history and do not track movement over time

Email communications:
• If enabled, emails are sent on days when the ISS is expected to be visible from your saved location
• Emails may also be sent to inform you about new versions, feature releases, or important updates to the application
• All emails are plain text only
• No sponsored, promotional, marketing, or third-party content is ever included
• You may disable email notifications at any time from your profile

What we do NOT do:
• We do not track you in real time
• We do not continuously access your location
• We do not access your contacts, files, or browsing activity
• We do not sell, share, or monetize your data in any form
• We do not use third-party analytics or profiling tools

Your control:
You remain in full control of your data at all times. You may update or delete your stored location, delete your saved user data, or permanently delete your account whenever you choose.
